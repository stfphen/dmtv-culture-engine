# DMTV Content Strategy & Engine Build Spec

*How the pages that win at scale actually operate — and how DMTV turns that into a defensible, high-value system. Researched June 2026 from Montreality, 6ixBuzz, The Shade Room, WorldStar, Overheard, RapTV, Highsnobiety, SSENSE, NTS, Boiler Room, Aimé Leon Dore, plus Meta/Mosseri ranking statements, a Nature study on negativity, and US/Canada copyright law. Sources at the end.*

---

## The one idea everything hangs on

**Owned, original capture is the through-line that unites all three goals at once:** it's the premium-brand differentiator, it's the cleanest copyright position, and it's what Instagram's algorithm now actively rewards. Every successful "high-value" culture brand we studied — Montreality, Highsnobiety, SSENSE, Aimé Leon Dore — wins on first-party content, not reposts. Every page that scaled on reposting (FuckJerry, and to a degree 6ixBuzz) hit a legal and reputational ceiling.

This matters *now* because of a hard platform change: **in April 2026 Instagram began removing reposted photos and carousels from all recommendation surfaces, and stated that adding a watermark or changing video speed does NOT count as transformation** (TechCrunch, 2026-04-30). The low-effort repost engine is no longer just risky — it's algorithmically dead. DMTV's instinct to stay "high-value" is now also the only thing that gets reach.

So the strategy is not "aggregate faster." It is: **be a media brand that produces a small amount of owned/transformed content and fragments it into many native assets, on a repeatable calendar, with a disciplined visual system.** The engine's job shifts from "package a link" to "run a culture desk."

---

## What the winners actually do (the five transferable mechanics)

**1. One source → many assets (the Montreality move).** Montreality's cyphers are deliberately shot so "each rapper is filmed solo with unique visuals" — one shoot day yields one hero video plus N self-contained vertical clips, at no extra production cost. The general model (GaryVee's content pyramid): one pillar (interview, drop, event) becomes ~10–30 micro-assets — clips, quote cards, a carousel, a mood image, a recap, an audiogram — each over-indexed for its platform. Then a **Listen loop**: read the comments, cut a second round from whatever resonated. Most teams skip the loop; it's free reach.

**2. Named, repeatable franchises on a calendar.** Overheard turned one repeatable unit (a quote on a branded card) into ~5M followers across a city-by-city network. 6ixBuzz ran a small set of recurring templates. The discipline is 4–6 *named slots* you run weekly, not bespoke posts. Templated production, distinct DMTV framing each time.

**3. A house-style overlay system — but beyond a watermark.** The thing that makes content read as *theirs* at a glance is a fixed type + color-block + placement + voice system. Critically, post-April-2026 a watermark alone is explicitly "low-effort" and won't earn reach — the house style has to carry actual editorial framing (a take, context, a ranking, a Toronto angle).

**4. Format chosen for the *signal* it manufactures.** Mosseri confirmed (Jan 2025) the top IG signals are watch time, then **sends-per-reach** (DM shares — "the most powerful signal for reaching new audiences"), then likes. So pick format by intent:
- **Sends / shares** (reach to new people) → quote cards, hot takes, before/after, identity-signaling images.
- **Saves** (durable relevance) → ranked lists, reference carousels, mood grids.
- **Threaded comments** (now weighted over one-word replies) → tier lists, brackets, "who won" debates.

**5. Premium voice = restraint + insider authority.** A Nature study (2023, 105K headlines / 5.7M clicks) found each negative word lifted click-through ~2.3% — tension sells. But Meta down-ranks engagement bait ("tag a friend," "like if you agree"), with an explicit exception for genuinely asking opinions. The resolution the luxury/culture brands use: **CTA as invitation, not command** ("Step inside" / "Read the story" / "Who should we watch next?"), proof through craft and insider detail instead of hype, and contrarian hooks only when they read as real insider knowledge rather than a template. Highsnobiety's register is literally "insider authority + accessibility."

## The DMTV operating system

### A. Franchise slate (the recurring formats)

Replace ad-hoc posting with a fixed slate. Each is templated, each carries DMTV framing, each maps to a target signal.

| Franchise | Cadence | Format | Target signal | DMTV angle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Culture Radar** | daily | news card / short clip | sends | fast, factual, DMTV-first on the update |
| **DMTV Take** | 3×/wk | quote/hot-take card | sends + comments | original commentary on a move (the brand-voice play) |
| **Before It Pops** | weekly | carousel / clip | saves | emerging Toronto artist/brand/event discovery |
| **Rate This Fit / Fit Check** | weekly | image + overlay | comments | streetwear, event footage (owned or permissioned) |
| **Who Ran The City** | weekly (Mon) | carousel recap | saves + sends | weekend nightlife/event recap |
| **Weekend Map** | weekly (Thu) | carousel / list | saves | upcoming events guide |
| **Best Toronto Verse / Bars** | weekly | clip + quote card | comments | local rap spotlight (permission-first) |
| **Aesthetic Breakdown** | weekly | mood carousel | saves | the visual language behind a drop/video/campaign |
| **Tier List / Bracket** | monthly | interactive carousel | comments | engineered, good-faith debate (no outrage farming) |

The line DMTV must hold: 6ixBuzz proved divisive comment-baiting scales reach but caps brand value (recalled Foot Locker collab, sustained criticism). DMTV competes on taste — debate prompts, never community-pitting.

### B. The repurposing pipeline (one item → an asset set)

For every strong source item or owned shoot, the engine should generate a **package**, not a single post:

1. **Hero angle** (the editorial thesis)
2. **3 caption variants** per target platform (IG / TikTok / Shorts / X / Story)
3. **A quote card** (sends)
4. **A carousel outline** — cover-slide hook + 5–9 value slides (saves)
5. **A mood/aesthetic image** (saves) — owned or licensed visual
6. **A clip brief** — if video exists, the 5–10 vertical cut-points to pull
7. **Rights posture** per asset (owned / permissioned / licensed / reference-only)

Rule of production: generate 5–8 drafts from one source, **publish the best over 2–3 weeks** — curate, don't dump. One good interview = a week of distribution.

### C. Visual system (premium, not cheap-repost)

From the designer interviews (Highsnobiety/SSENSE/NTS/Boiler Room/ALD), the tells of "premium" are consistent: **restraint over loudness, a neutral/owned type system, original capture over aggregation, ruthless consistency, white space, and a reduced logo as an authenticity stamp.** DMTV's deck already lives here — black, heavy Helvetica caps, Poppins, the gold lightning mark. The system to lock:

- **Type:** one heading face (Helvetica/Akzidenz-grade bold caps), Poppins for body. No third font, ever.
- **Color discipline:** black + off-white + gold accent. Gold is a *spark*, not a fill.
- **Photographic treatment:** documentary, flash-photography nightlife energy — real Toronto scenes, graded consistently. "Reality is the ultimate art director" (SSENSE).
- **Logo as a mark of authenticity** — small, consistent placement; let content quality carry it (NTS principle).
- **Motion:** define how the wordmark behaves over footage (Boiler Room's identity "came into its own in motion").
- **Locked templates / brand tokens** so anyone can produce on-brand at 9pm without a designer.

### D. Rights posture (Canada-specific — this is not legal advice)

Canada's fair dealing is a **closed list** (research, private study, education, parody, satire, criticism, review, news reporting) with **no general "it's transformative" catch-all** (CCH, 2004 SCC 13). Purely promotional reposting of party/fashion footage to build a brand feed generally fits **none** of those purposes. Credit never cures infringement. So DMTV's defaults:

1. **Permission-first** for any third-party clip/photo — written, scoped (platforms, organic vs. paid, edit rights, duration). A "love this!" comment is not consent.
2. **Owned/commissioned shoots** with model/property releases — note real party/fashion footage has identifiable people and needs *commercial* licensing + releases, not editorial stock.
3. **Licensed fill** — commercially-licensed stock; for music use **commercial libraries** (TikTok CML, Meta Sound Collection, Epidemic Sound), never the consumer catalog.
4. **Reference-only** for anything uncleared — used to brief an *original* DMTV asset, never republished.

This is the same conclusion as the brand argument: owned capture is both the premium move and the clean-rights move.

### E. Cadence & distribution

Treat **Reels + TikTok + Shorts as one distribution layer** — once the vertical is cut, cross-posting is nearly free. YouTube/long-form is the catalog/SEO anchor; IG is the social hub. Batch production by stage (script all week, shoot back-to-back, edit in one block). Bursts around access (events, drops) beat a rigid daily grind, but the franchise calendar guarantees a floor.

## How the engine changes (build spec)

The current engine does: source → summarize → score → 3 ideas → captions → 1 graphic. Here's the prioritized upgrade path to make it run like a culture desk. Each maps to files in this repo.

### Priority 1 — Editorial intelligence (biggest quality jump)
1. **Asset-package generator.** Replace "3 ideas + 1 graphic" with a full package per item (hero angle, quote card, carousel outline, mood image, clip brief, per-platform captions). → new `lib/culture-engine/generatePackage.js`; extend `culture_content_ideas` with an `asset_type` and a parent `package_id`.
2. **Franchise tagging + calendar.** Encode the franchise slate; the scorer routes each item to its best-fit franchise(s) and the dashboard gets a **Content Calendar** view. → extend `score.js`/new `franchises.js`; new `culture_calendar` table; new dashboard tab.
3. **Signal-aware formats.** Tag every generated asset with its target signal (send/save/comment) and choose format accordingly. → prompt + schema field `target_signal`.
4. **Hook engine + premium-voice guardrails.** Rotate 8–10 proven hook formulas; bake the restraint/insider-authority rules and the "invitation-not-command" CTA list into the caption/idea prompts; ban engagement-bait phrasing. → `prompts.js` rewrite.
5. **Real AI on.** All of the above only sings with the Anthropic key set — the mock heuristics are the "low-effort" feel you flagged.

### Priority 2 — Visual output that reads premium
6. **Carousel renderer.** Multi-slide SVG/PNG output: cover-slide hook + value slides, consistent system. → extend `renderTemplate.js`; new `/api/render/[ideaId]/carousel`.
7. **PNG/JPG export** (scheduler-ready) via `@resvg/resvg-js` or `sharp`. → `/api/render/[ideaId].png`.
8. **Quote-card + mood-grid templates** locked to the type/color system.
9. **Owned-capture-first visual weighting** (already built) + a "needs original shoot" recommendation when no clean visual exists.

### Priority 3 — Operating the desk
10. **Clip-brief module** for video sources (timestamped cut-points to pull) — pairs with CapCut/Opus/Submagic in the human workflow.
11. **Listen loop** — capture per-post engagement (manual entry or API) and feed winners back into next week's ideas + source `priority_score`.
12. **Permission tracker** — per-asset rights status, submission/permission log, paper trail.
13. **Franchise scheduler** — auto-build next week's calendar from the highest-scoring items per slot.

---

## What this is NOT

Not an auto-poster. Not a repost wall (algorithmically dead as of April 2026). Not an outrage/engagement-bait machine (caps brand value). Not a fair-dealing gamble on other people's footage. It's a **culture desk that surfaces 10–20 genuinely worth-posting, DMTV-voiced, rights-clean packages a week** — and increasingly pushes DMTV toward owning the capture, which is the only durable moat.
